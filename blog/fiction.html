Fiction Review: Clytemnestra's Last Day by S. Montana Katz

Following on the heels of similar reinterpretations of ancient Greek heroines such as Alcestis (Katharine Beutner, Alcestis: a novel) or Penelope (Margaret Atwood, The Penelopiad), Clytemnestra's Last Day seeks to continue the trend of reimagining Greek myths in modern feminist contexts.

Fiction Review: The Girl Who Was Saturday Night by Heather O'Neill

Despite its backdrop and its Quebec-born author, The Girl Who Was Saturday Night does not try to challenge any stereotypes about Quebec and, in fact, seems to validate some of them. Then again, O'Neill has depicted here isn't your typical tourist brochure Montreal; this is the Montreal of a particular sect, the proud and the lost and the disillusioned, people like Nicolas and Nouschka Tremblay.
2016/05/27 | Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ½
